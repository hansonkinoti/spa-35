Essential Etiquette Tips for a Stress-Free Visit

Arrive Early: Arrive at the spa at least 15-30 minutes prior to your appointment to allow enough time to change into a robe, fill out forms and relax before your treatment. 

Be Respectful:

Be respectful of other guests and spa staff, use low voices and be mindful of your phone usage. Be Open-Minded: Be open to new spa experiences and treatments, but also remember to listen to your body and speak up if you’re not comfortable with anything.

Communication is Key:

Clearly communicate your expectations and preferences to the therapist before your treatment. This includes any areas you would like them to avoid, pressure preference, etc. 

Dress Appropriately:

 Most spas require you to wear a robe or swimwear for treatments, but some may have specific dress codes, so make sure to check before your appointment. Know the Products: If you have sensitive skin or allergies, make sure to inform the therapist and ask about the products being used before your treatment. 

Gratuities:

Tipping is generally expected in spas, so it’s a good idea to bring cash for a gratuity at the end of your visit.

Respect Quiet Zones: Spas are meant to be relaxing and peaceful, so respect quiet zones and avoid talking on your phone or using other devices in these areas. 

Respect Your Therapist:

Be respectful of your therapist and their work, let them know if you’re uncomfortable or in pain during the treatment. Spa Cancellation Policy: Most spas have a strict cancellation policy, so make sure to check the policy before scheduling your appointment and give proper notice if you need to cancel.
